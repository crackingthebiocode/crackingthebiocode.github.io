Julia Palacios

Postdoctoral Researcher
Harvard University
Organismic and Evolutionary Biology
Brown University
Ecology and Evolutionary Biology

Website: Julia Palacios

What inspired you to start research in computational biology?

To learn about past prevalence of infectious diseases from genomic data sampled today. Genomic data provide a unique opportunity to retrospective studies.

What are 5 important skills needed for your research?

  • Stochastic Process and Inference of Stochastic process
  • Computing in R, python, Java, C

How did you learn the computational and quantitative skills necessary for your work?

I did a Masters and a PhD in statistics with focus on statistical genetics. I participated in statistical genetics summer courses and high throughput genomics workshops. I have worked with excellent mentors with expertise in the area. In terms of programming and databases, I am self-trained and I keep learning everyday.

What important questions do you address in your research?

Population Demographic History and population level parameters that can be learned from genomic data and from a combination of genomic data and other sources of information.
